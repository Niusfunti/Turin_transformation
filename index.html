01 — The Dataset

206 sites, tracked since 2020

Since July 2020, the Visitul project has systematically documented every major urban transformation site in Turin — former industrial zones, historic buildings, public spaces under conversion.

Across seven snapshots over five years, each site was assigned a status. Together, they form a data portrait of a city in slow, uneven change.

02 — The Colours

Four stages of change

Every site moves — or doesn't — through a pipeline:

On hold Procedure in progress Under construction Completed

The map shows where every tracked site stood in December 2025. Most of the city is still waiting.

03 — The Wait

42 sites on hold

Nearly a third of tracked polygon sites remain "on hold" as of December 2025 — frozen in planning, ownership disputes, or political limbo.

Some have been documented as stalled since the very first snapshot in July 2020, five years without movement.

04 — The Pipeline

43 in the machine

A near-equal number are navigating bureaucratic procedures — permits, environmental reviews, public consultations. They've started, but nothing is yet built.

In Italian urban planning, this phase can take years. Some sites cycle in and out of it repeatedly.

05 — The Build

48 under construction

The largest single group in December 2025: sites where work has physically begun — cranes, scaffolding, excavations across the city.

This is the highest construction count across all seven snapshots: a late surge after years of procedural delay.

06 — The Finish Line

Only 11 completed

Across five years and 150 tracked polygon sites, just eleven have reached completion. Urban transformation, it turns out, is an exercise in patience.

Five more sites were removed from tracking entirely — demolished, repurposed, or their transformation abandoned.

07 — July 2020

Where it began

In the first snapshot, the map is almost entirely grey. The pandemic year: nearly every site documented, almost none moving. Turin had named its problems; it had not yet begun solving them.

08 — July 2022

The tide turns

Two years later, amber starts to spread — procedures launched, permits filed. A handful of orange patches mark construction that has actually started. The post-pandemic recovery brought renewed energy to planning departments.

09 — December 2025

The city today

By late 2025, orange dominates — cranes and scaffolding across the metropolitan area. But grey remains stubbornly present. Not every site found its momentum.

10 — A Success Story

Mercato dei Fiori

The former flower market (N031) is one of just eleven sites to make it all the way through the pipeline. On hold in 2020, procedures began in late 2021, construction started in December 2022 — and by late 2025, it was done.

11 — An Unresolved Saga

Monteverdi

Site N067 holds the record: four status changes across five years. It climbed from "on hold" to "procedure in progress," fell back, rose again — and ended 2025 exactly where it started: on hold.

A portrait of bureaucratic uncertainty in miniature.
